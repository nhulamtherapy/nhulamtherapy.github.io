A Note From Your Therapist 


For many individuals, therapy is a scary place filled with unknowns. You will be sitting with a complete stranger expecting to share your deepest thoughts and feelings. I thought it would ease some clients to understand what therapy may look like with me.


My approach to therapy is warm and collaborative. My clinical framework draws from humanistic and existential thought. What is most important through these frameworks is to explore the world of each client. What makes you, you.Thus, it is important as your therapist that I provide a space where a client’s self-discovery can grow. I encouraged each of my clients to continuously guide me to create a space where you feel comfortable and safe.


How to prepare for each therapy session is to reflect on what areas in your life that you want to dive deeper. What makes therapy unique is it is an external space to explore internal topics, so no topic is a silly topic.


The relationship between the therapist and client is an on-going process filled with learning and re-examining. Like any relationship, the therapeutic relationship will take time to build, so bear that in mind as we get to know each other. My belief is through the relationship between the therapist and client, the client will take a curious stance on who one is with oneself and others. This is where the work begins.The journey begins, like the curious paradox states, when one accepts oneself just as one is, then one can change.


Entry to therapy can hold many fears and worries for individuals, but nonetheless you are here and that is worth celebrating. I want to welcome you to your journey! Welcome!
